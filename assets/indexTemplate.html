Fourier

Explanations

A Fourier series is an expansion of a periodic function f(x) in terms of an infinite sum of sines and cosines. (Wolfram MathWorld) Three functions are illustrated:

  • Box function: f0(x) = 1, x ∈ [-π, 0] and f0(x) = 0, x ∈ [0, π]
  • Linear function: f1(x) = x, x ∈ [-π, π]
  • Quadratic function: f2(x) = x2, x ∈ [-π, π]
